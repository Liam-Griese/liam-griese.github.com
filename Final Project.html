Ideas and Consultation

I came up with three different ideas for my final project: A large wooden calendar, a cribbage board, an a frame for a photograph. The Calender would be a large grid that wold be cut into a piece of wood. There would be a large number of tiles that would fit into these spaces in the grid. Each of the tiles would have a number on it 1-31. One could rearrange the tiles to reflect any given month. The Cribbage board idea would be a simple cribbage board that would be cut using the shaper and then engraved with a cool design using the laser cutter. I would then design and print 3d pegs to uses as the pieces. The third idea was to make a cool photo frame that had fancy designs engraves on it. At the consultation I discussed the three ideas and I ended up deciding to go with the Cribbage board idea. Of all of the ideas this was the most personal to me. Me and my family play a lot of cribbage and so it would be used a lot at home. Also my Great-Uncle used to make cribbage boards, so I think it would be really fun to do the same. I decided as well that I would make the Cribbage board a Christmas gift for my sister. The three skeches are attached below.

First Prototype

I began the process of designing my cribbage board by making a canvas that was the size of the piece of wood the shaper would be able to handle. I knew that the board I could make would have to be smaller than that. Then I started to make the holes that would go into the Cribbage board. There are 120 holes in a standard board and the smallest I would be able to make them would be 1/4 of an inch, becasue that is the size of the shaper bit. Next I put 30 holes in a row across the width of the board, making sure that all of the holes were the same size and equal distrance from one another. Then I copied the 30 holes and pasted then thrice above, so that there were 4 rows of 30 holes. My final design will be wih the shaper, but the first iteration of my design, I made it with the laser cutter. I did ths so I could see how the 1/4 holes would look and how the board looked when it was printed. The laser cutter allowed me to do with quickly and effectively. Photos of the first prototype are below.

Redesign of the Board

After making the laser cut first protoype to see how the holes would look, I realized that I wanted to make a more complex design. I basically totally restarted in Fusion 360.

I wanted to go for a design where the holes were in a triangle pattern and weren't just in a row. This would allow me to reduce the distance that holes would take up, while making sure I could still have the holes be 1/4 inch apart from one another. I statred by designing one set of the triangle. The center of the holes were .50 apart and along a 45 degree angle.

Once I made one set of the new pattern I could simply copy and paste it, until I had made a full row.

Then I simply mirrored the design over a line 1/4 inch below the first set of holes. This would mean that the second row's hoels would be 1/2 inch from the center of the first row.

Then I mirrored the whole design over the center of the board, this way it woudl be on the top and the bottom. After I did that, I went back throught the design and began to delete all the extra junk, such as the lines that I used to make sure the holes were the correct distance apart. I should have done this earlier, before I mirrored it a bunch of times, but I didn't. This meant that it took a good while to get rid of all the lines.

Next I put it into illustrator. So that I could color the parts the right way so that the Shaper will know how to cut the holes.

Cutting the Second Prototype

I used the shaper to cut out the 120 holes for the cribbage board and cut the board out. Once the bribbage board was cut out, I added a laser-cut engraving on the back. I stuck a silly one on the back of this version, because it isn't the fianlly version. The final version will have the date I made it and likey will not have Jackson's face on it. Moving forward, I will want to add some more fancy elements to the board. for example, if the tops of the holes are tapered so they feel really nice. I will also want to move the rows of holes around so they are a little more centered.
