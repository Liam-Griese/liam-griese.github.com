What is FDM Printing

FDM Printing is a form of printing which has an extruder adds layers of plastic on top of one another, making a design. The machine is feed a length of filiment, which is heated and melted so it can be extruded. It slowly makes the design up, building it layer by layer on the print bed. This process can take a very long time.

Prusa Slicer

Prusa Slicer is the program which converts the files downloaded from Fusion 360 to G-code, which can be read by the 3D printers. The program allows you to place your model in a model of the printer and scale it to the correct size. The program also has a bunch of Customization for different types of filiment and quality of prints.

Designing Process

I had a design that I had made before the one you see above, but the stupid program crashed so I had to start again. The first step in the design process was to make a circle that would fit inside of the circle shaped hole in my desk organizer. I made it a bit smaller than the actual circle cut out, that way I could be sure that it would fit. Next I extruded the circle the height of the indent and then added a short cylinder on top to form the bottom of the vase. After that I made a few offset planes above the cylinder and connected when with the loft tool. Then I used the shell tool to make the whole design hollow, and a vase. After that I saved the design and headed to print the design! It took around 12 hours to print
